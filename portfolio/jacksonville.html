Urban Heat Islands in Jacksonville, Florida

This map was for a final capstone project of Geography 578, also called GIS Applications. My partner and I, Ronald Kelley, were interested in urban heat effects cities, as well as natural or man-made management strategies to counteract heat islands. Jacksonville, Florida, was chosen as the site of interest due to it's high levels of open concrete, presence of a military base, high levels of racial segregation and wealth disparity, as well as bounty of marshlands surrounding the city. We also chose to use user-submitted data through the Citizen Weather Observer Program to develop a heat map of the city, then factored in all of the previous factors to determine what creates heat islands or heat sinks around the city.
